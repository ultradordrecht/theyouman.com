13-02-16 Baracca: Inside Job: The Director's cut LocatieZ Den Haag
works by: Kees Koomen, Pim Voorneman, Herman Lamers, Twan Bastiaansen, Christine Gist, Andrew McNiven, Marleen Oud, Tanja Smit, Anthony Blokdijk, Norman Maus, Thijs Ebbe Fokkens, Max Kuiper, Matthias König, Alette Wttewaall, Gast Bouschet & Nadine Hilbert, Mercedes Langhorst, Helena Sanders, Dorina Kappatou, Frans van Lent

DEN HAAG, 2015
The brief window of time that allowed artists to run independent galleries on A-locations in
the city centre for little or no rent at all, was just that: a brief moment. A privilege, although achieved at the
price of a bland cultural landscape, and of a gradual erosion of the will to take risks, to experiment.
We lost our exhibition space about half a decade ago, but the conviction and desire to share the art that inspires us has only increased.
In 2015, we intend to tour Europe to organise brief exhibitions at the tables, bars or in rooms of restaurants, pubs, hotels that also bear the name Baracca, or variations thereupon.
Not because we are neo-Situationists or to wave the punk DIY flag, or to make any kind of political statement, but because we have something to show you.
We have something to show you.

BLACK MASS
August 7th
15:00-21:00
monsterseweg 4
The Hague

Black Mass descends into the Chthonic realm of the human psyche,
exploring the rites and theories that contemporary [sub]cultures employ
to confront the concept of Darkness.

featuring work by
Terence Hannum, Alette Wttewaal, Basten Rolf Stokhuyzen, Thijs Ebbe Fokkens
And Baracca, with
Alexander Binder, Barney de Krijger, Floris Kaayk, Frans van Lent, Ibrahim R. Ineke,
Marcel Ruijters, Paul Klaui, Yvo van der Vat

And a musical performance by
Selwyn Slop & Bart Schotman.
and
Mihailo Cubrovic

will present a concise elucidation of the functioning of black holes
in order to arrive at a definition of the concept
of the black hole as a useful model in artistic practice.

Prof. Niall Scott

will extemporize upon the subject of blackening, darkness and
void in contemporary musical subculture, its ritual,
significance and tradition.
